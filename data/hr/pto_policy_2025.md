# Time Off and Leave Policy

## Overview

This document outlines time off and leave policies for employees at
Acme Payments. It includes vacation time, sick leave, and other types
of approved absence.

This policy is intended as a general guide and will be administered in
accordance with applicable federal, state, and local laws.

---

## Eligibility

This policy applies to full-time employees.

- part-time employees receive prorated benefits based on hours worked
- temporary employees may be eligible for sick leave where required by law
- independent contractors are not covered unless specified in their agreement

---

## Paid Time Off (PTO)

Employees receive paid time off for vacation and personal use.

### Accrual

- employees accrue 15 days of PTO per year
- accrual begins on the employee start date
- PTO is accrued monthly

### Usage

PTO may be used for:

- vacation
- personal matters
- short-term illness

New hires may begin using PTO after 90 days of employment.

---

## Sick Leave

Sick leave is provided in accordance with applicable law.

- employees receive at least 5 days of paid sick leave per year
- accrual and usage are subject to local regulations

Sick leave may be used for:

- personal illness
- medical appointments
- caring for a family member

---

## Carryover

- employees may carry over up to 5 unused PTO days
- unused PTO above this limit is forfeited

---

## Parental Leave

### Maternity Leave

- eligible employees may take up to 12 weeks of leave

### Paternity or Partner Leave

- eligible employees may take up to 2 weeks of leave

Leave may be paid or unpaid depending on eligibility and applicable law.

---

## Bereavement Leave

Employees may take up to 3 days of leave for the death of an immediate
family member.

---

## Jury Duty Leave

Employees are allowed time off for jury duty.

- this leave is unpaid
- documentation is required

---

## Holidays

Company holidays are observed separately and do not count against PTO.

---

## Approval Guidelines

- PTO requests should be submitted through the HR system

- requests for 3 or more consecutive days should be submitted at least
  2 weeks in advance

- requests may be denied during critical business periods

---

## Termination and Payout

Accrued unused PTO will be paid out upon termination where required by law.

---

## Notes

- PTO balances are tracked in the HR system
- employees are encouraged to plan time off in advance
